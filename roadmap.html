Financial Planning · Outcomes · Support

From investment to self-sustaining impact.

The financial strategy follows "early investment, mid-term industry balance, long-term self-sufficiency," raising operating funds through diverse channels.

Funding

Four pillars of funding

Start-up fund

University appropriation or fundraising for initial build-out: administrative staffing, core lab fit-out, and high-end GPU servers co-built with the computing center.

Government competitive grants

NSTC "AI CoE," MOE "Higher Education Sprout Project," and MOEA "frontier technology R&D", supporting post-doc salaries.

Industry & tech-transfer income

Joint-lab entry fees, project development funds, and AI4X patent licensing, the main growth driver as technology matures.

Donations & incubation returns

Donations from distinguished alumni and entrepreneurs, plus a feedback mechanism with incubated AI start-ups.

Roadmap

A three-phase financial roadmap

Year 1–2 Start-up phaseFocus on hardware build-out, funded mainly by university appropriation and government equipment grants. Build
Year 3–4 Growth phaseFocus on winning grants; large integrated research projects fund operations, with early industry collaboration launched. Grow
Year 5+ Maturity phaseFocus on industry sustainability; tech transfer and commissioned research rise significantly, building a self-sufficient cycle. Sustain
Outcomes

Expected outcomes within 3–5 years

◦ Academic excellence

Research & international reputation

  • Top publications at NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR and cross-domain flagship journals every year.
  • Successful large integrated AI grants from NSTC or cross-ministry programs annually.
  • Higher global standing in CSRankings / QS for AI and cross-domain research.
◦ Industrial impact

Co-creation & economic benefit

  • Annual technology transfer cases driving industrial derivative investment.
  • Three-year joint labs with flagship enterprises.
  • Incubated AI start-ups securing seed or Series A funding.
◦ Talent cultivation

Cross-domain talent & education

  • 500+ students per year completing AI4X micro-programs with digital certificates.
  • 30+ master's & PhD specialists per year in frontier or cross-domain AI.
  • At least 5 AI workshops per year for faculty, staff, and local enterprises.
◦ Social & policy

Governance & policy contribution

  • An "AI Ethics & Rule-of-Law White Paper" for Taiwan's industry, with standing advisory status.
  • 2–3 AI systems improving public-service efficiency (e.g. smart clinic scheduling).
  • 1 campus-level data-sharing platform with 10+ high-quality feature datasets.
Coordination

Support measures across the university

Academic Affairs & colleges

Joint-appointment and promotion mechanisms: flexible faculty positions, recognition of cross-domain AI work in promotion, and simplified cross-college credit transfer for micro-programs.

General Affairs & computing

Space allocation and environment integration, power/cooling reinforcement, campus backbone upgrade (100 Gbps+), and a shared computing reservation & scheduling system.

R&D & innovation office

A patent "green channel" with IP strategy consulting, plus active enterprise matchmaking and contract/compliance support.

Secretariat & PR

International promotion support (visas, MoUs) and turning major results into press releases to raise the university's AI brand.
