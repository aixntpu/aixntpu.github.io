Organizational Structure

Flat administration, cross-domain academics.

The center adopts a "flat administration, cross-domain academics" design, with a Steering Committee as the strategic decision core and an executive layer that implements operations.

Org chart

Figure 1 · Center organizational structure

Highest decision body · convened by the President

Center Director & Technical Director

Executive leadership · daily operations and strategy

TRL 3–7 translation · joint labs · spin-offs

Micro-programs · AI ethics & governance research

Top international cross-domain AI scholars · evaluation & consulting

◦ 1

Steering Committee

The highest decision body, responsible for development direction, resource allocation, and performance evaluation.

  • Convener: the President — symbolizing strategic height.
  • Deputy convener: a Vice President (administrative or academic).
  • Members: the VP for R&D, college deans (law, business, public affairs, social science, humanities, sustainability, ECE), distinguished scholars, and flagship industry leaders.
◦ 2

Executive Leadership

Responsible for daily operations and strategy execution.

  • Center Director: a professor of international academic standing with cross-domain integration ability, appointed by the President.
  • Technical Director: one position, leading technical R&D on a dual track.
◦ 3

Functional Divisions

A "task organization" jointly staffed by the center's full-time members and faculty jointly appointed with departments.

  • Industrial Technology: TRL 3–7 translation into smart manufacturing, healthcare, sustainability, and communications.
  • Cross-Domain Application: the core of AI4X, with technical managers as academic translators.
  • Education & Innovation: AI4X micro-programs plus AI ethics, law, and governance research.
◦ 4

Advisory Board

Top international cross-domain AI scholars serve as evaluation and advisory members, regularly providing strategies to improve research and governance.
